OUR GOAL


We at TerraHacks aim to provide high schoolers an avenue to demonstrate their creativity and skill by coming up with real solutions to real problems that our world faces today. Our tracks aim to guide our attendees to these global issues, and our workshops and mentors aim to provide the knowledge needed to bring their ideas to reality.

FAQ


WHAT IS TERRAHACKS?


TerraHacks is a virtual hackathon hosted by students where both new and experienced programmers can work together on projects, learn new skills, and have a splendid time. Our events are backed by HackClub, a 501 (c)(3) public charity registered in CA. All donations are tax - deductible. Please feel free to email us at hello@terrahacks.com if you have any questions or concerns.

WHAT IS A HACKATHON?

A Hackathon is a 24 to 36 hour long coding marathon event where people can make the most bizarre ideas into reality. Over the course of the weekend, students collaborate to create projects, listen to exquisite workshop presentations, exercise their programming abilities, and of course, have tons of fun!

TIME AND LOCATION?

TerraHacks will be taking place in July (exact date TBD). Since our hackathon is virtual, we welcome students from anywhere in the world to participate!

DO I NEED PROGRAMMING EXPERIENCE?

No! In fact, TerraHacks is a great way to begin your programming experience! A basic understanding is recommended, but we will have workshops and mentors to help you make your project idea a reality.

WHAT WILL I NEED?

You will need a computer, a valid student ID (or some sort of student ID), and any supplies you need for your hack.

WHO CAN ATTEND?

This event is open to high schoolers ONLY. No middle schoolers will be allowed. However, college students may sign up to mentor, volunteer, or lead a workshop. Sign up here, or email hello@terrahacks.com if you have any questions or concerns.

DO I NEED TO WORK OVERNIGHT?

Since TerraHacks is virtual, it’s up to you to decide when to work on your hack.

TEAM REQUIREMENTS?

You need at least 1 person per team and 4 people at most.

DO I NEED TO SUBMIT TO A TRACK?

No; the tracks are meant to provide general paths to relevant problems attendees may want to solve. There will also be track-specific prizes along with overall prizes. If you chose to not follow one of the tracks, you will still have the opportunity to win one of these overall prizes.
